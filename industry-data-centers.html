Data Center Economic Impact Studies

How We Help

Data center investments are among the largest capital projects in the country, generating thousands of construction jobs, significant ongoing employment, and substantial tax revenue for host communities. Whether you are a hyperscale operator seeking zoning approval, a developer applying for tax incentives, or a local government evaluating a proposed project, an economic impact study provides the credible, independent data that decision-makers need.

We work with operators, developers, utilities, and government agencies on site-specific and portfolio-level studies. Our analysis covers both the construction phase and long-term operations, with detailed breakdowns by tax type and government jurisdiction, built to support public hearings, incentive applications, and stakeholder communication.

Methodology

What We Measure

Construction Impact

Jobs, wages, and output from site preparation, building construction, and equipment installation.

Operational Impact

Ongoing employment, utility purchases, maintenance, and supply chain spending during facility operations.

Tax Revenue

Property taxes, sales taxes, income taxes, and other fiscal contributions at every government level.

Induced Effects

Household spending by construction workers and operations employees rippling through the local economy.

Supply Chain Analysis

Upstream spending on electrical equipment, cooling systems, fiber, and professional services.

Community Investment

Workforce training programs, infrastructure improvements, and community benefit commitments.
