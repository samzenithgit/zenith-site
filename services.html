Core Service

Economic Impact Analysis

We quantify how your organization influences a regional economy using IMPLAN input-output modeling. Our team also has hands-on experience with REMI, RIMS II, Lightcast, Chmura, and Eora Global MRIO, allowing us to select the right tool for every engagement. As independent economists, our analysis is objective and methodologically transparent, built to withstand scrutiny from regulators, legislators, and the press. Every study measures: jobs supported, labor income, GDP/GRP, economic output, and fiscal impacts by tax type and government level.

We use Multi-Regional Input-Output (MRIO) models when studies span multiple geographies, capturing commuting and trade patterns.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does an economic impact study cost?

Costs vary based on scope, geography, data requirements, and methodology. We provide fixed-fee quotes for every engagement, so there are no surprises. Contact us for a detailed proposal tailored to your project.

How long does an economic impact study take?

Most studies are completed in 6 to 12 weeks depending on data availability and scope. For urgent needs, we can deliver a study in as little as one to two weeks on an expedited basis. Proposals with detailed timelines are delivered within 48 hours of your inquiry.

What is IMPLAN and why does it matter?

IMPLAN is one of the leading input-output modeling platforms used to measure how economic activity ripples through a regional economy. It quantifies direct, indirect, and induced effects across jobs, income, output, and tax revenues. Our team includes two IMPLAN Certified Economists and also has experience with REMI, RIMS II, Lightcast, Chmura, and Eora Global MRIO, allowing us to select the right tool for each engagement.

What deliverables are included?

Our studies can include a professionally designed final report (PDF), an executive summary, a presentation deck (PowerPoint), a formatted data workbook (Excel), and in-person board presentations. Deliverables are scoped to each engagement and detailed in your proposal.

Why does independence matter in economic impact analysis?

Legislators, journalists, and stakeholders scrutinize both methodology and motive. As an independent firm with no financial interest in the outcomes we study, our findings carry more weight in public hearings, media coverage, and stakeholder presentations.

Do you work outside of California?

Yes. While we are based in Los Angeles, our work is nationwide. We have completed studies in Texas, Montana, Missouri, and across multiple other states. Our input-output models, including those done in IMPLAN and other tools, cover every U.S. county and ZIP code.
