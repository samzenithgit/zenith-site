University Economic Impact Studies

How We Help

Higher education institutions create enormous economic value that extends far beyond tuition revenue. From direct employment and campus operations to student spending, alumni earnings, and workforce development, your institution drives jobs, income, and tax revenue across the region. An economic impact study gives you the data to demonstrate that value to legislators, donors, accreditors, and the surrounding community.

Whether you are a community college making the case for state funding, a university seeking donor engagement, or a system office reporting to your board, we deliver independent, methodologically rigorous analysis designed for the audiences that matter most to your institution.

What We Measure

Jobs & Labor Income

Direct employment, faculty, staff, and contracted workers. Induced spending from employee households.

Student & Visitor Spending

Off-campus expenditures by students, families, and event attendees flowing into the local economy.

Fiscal Impact

Tax revenues generated at local, state, and federal levels, including property, sales, and income taxes.

Workforce Development

Alumni earnings premiums, credential attainment, and labor force participation effects.

Construction & Capital

Economic activity from campus construction, renovation, and capital investment programs.

Community Benefits

Healthcare services, cultural programming, volunteer hours, and other non-market contributions.
